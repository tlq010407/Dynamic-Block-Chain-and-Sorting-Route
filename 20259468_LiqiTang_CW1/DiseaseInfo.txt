---------------------------------------------------------
Malaria
Fever, chills, fatigue
Antimalarial drugs
---------------------------------------------------------
Coronary Heart Disease
Chest pain (angina), shortness of breath, fatigue
Lifestyle changes, medications, angioplasty, or bypass surgery
---------------------------------------------------------
Stroke
Sudden numbness or weakness in the face, arm, or leg; confusion; trouble speaking or understanding; severe headache
Emergency medical care, medications, physical therapy, and lifestyle changes
---------------------------------------------------------
Influenza & Pneumonia
Fever, cough, sore throat, chills, difficulty breathing (pneumonia)
Rest, fluids, antiviral medications (for influenza), antibiotics (for bacterial pneumonia)
---------------------------------------------------------
Lung Diseases
Varies depending on the specific lung disease but may include shortness of breath, coughing, wheezing, and chest pain
Treatment varies widely based on the specific lung disease and may include medications, oxygen therapy, or surgery
---------------------------------------------------------
Lung Cancer
Persistent cough, chest pain, coughing up blood, unexplained weight loss
Surgery, chemotherapy, radiation therapy, targeted therapy, immunotherapy
---------------------------------------------------------
Diabetes Mellitus
Increased thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, blurred vision
Lifestyle changes, blood sugar monitoring, medications (insulin or oral), and insulin therapy
---------------------------------------------------------
Breast Cancer
Lump in the breast, changes in breast size or shape, nipple discharge, breast pain
Surgery (mastectomy or lumpectomy), chemotherapy, radiation therapy, hormone therapy, targeted therapy
---------------------------------------------------------
Diabetes
Increased thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, blurred vision
Lifestyle changes (diet and exercise), blood sugar monitoring, medications (insulin or oral), and insulin therapy as needed.
---------------------------------------------------------
Hypertension
Often asymptomatic, but in severe cases, it can lead to headaches, shortness of breath, nosebleeds, and chest pain.
Lifestyle changes (diet, exercise, stress management), antihypertensive medications, and regular blood pressure monitoring.
---------------------------------------------------------
Rhinitis
Runny or stuffy nose, sneezing, itching, and nasal congestion. It can be caused by allergens (allergic rhinitis) or non-allergenic factors (non-allergic rhinitis).
Management includes allergen avoidance, antihistamines, decongestants, nasal corticosteroids, and immunotherapy (for allergic rhinitis).
---------------------------------------------------------
Dermatitis
Skin inflammation, redness, itching, and rashes. Dermatitis can vary in severity and may be triggered by allergies, irritants, or genetics.
Moisturizers, topical corticosteroids, antihistamines (for itching), and lifestyle modifications to avoid triggers.
---------------------------------------------------------
